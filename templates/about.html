About 4-Player Chess

Ancient variants meets modern technology

🏰 Project Overview

A modern, real-time multiplayer implementation of 4-player chess variants including Chaturaji (ancient Indian) and Enochian Chess (Victorian occult) with WebSocket synchronization and cybersecurity-focused architecture.

Built with ❤️ by Jon Cybersecurity • Gaming • Modern Web Tech

🕌 Chaturaji (Ancient Indian Variant)

📜 Historical Background

  • Originated in ancient India (6th-7th century)
  • Predecessor to modern chess
  • Mentioned in Sanskrit literature
  • Played by Indian royalty and nobility

🎯 Game Objective

Score the highest points by capturing enemy pieces. Game ends when one player loses all pieces or time expires.

⚡ Unique Rules

Scoring System: • King/Horse: 3 points • Elephant/Boat: 5 points • Pawn: 1 point Special Moves: • Boat: 2 squares diagonally (jumping) • Multiple king checks: +1 or +5 bonus • Dead armies remain on board

🔮 Enochian Chess (Victorian Occult Variant)

📚 Historical Background

  • Developed by the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn
  • Created in late 19th century England
  • Based on Enochian magical system
  • Used for divination and magical practice

🎯 Game Objective

Team-based gameplay: Blue/Black alliance vs Red/Yellow alliance. Win by capturing both enemy kings.

⚡ Unique Rules

Team Structure: • Blue & Black vs Red & Yellow • Coordinated team strategy required Special Moves: • Queen: Leaps exactly 2 squares • Throne squares: Double occupancy • Team victory conditions
